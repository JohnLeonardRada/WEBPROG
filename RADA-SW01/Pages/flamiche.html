FLAMICHE

Flamiche means ‘cake’ in Flemish and this dish originates from northern France, near the border with Belgium. It has a puff-pastry crust filled with cheese and vegetables and resembles a quiche. The traditional French filling is with leeks and cream. There is also a pizza-like version of flamiche, which is without the top crust of the pie. For a southern French twist, try the thin crusty pissaladière which is topped with anchovies, onions and olives.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 frozen pie crust
  • 3 cup leek
  • 1 egg
  • black pepper as required
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup low fat milk
  • salt as required

PROCEDURE

  • To make this snack recipe, preheat the oven to 200 degrees Celsius. On a chopping board, thinly slice the leeks and add these to a bowl. Place a pan over medium flame and melt butter in it. When the butter has melted, add the thinly sliced leeks to it and mix them well. Saute these for a while and let them get tender. once done, take it off the flame.
  • Now, crack open the eggs in a bowl and beat them. Add the milk in the bowl containing the eggs and season with salt and black pepper powder. Now, add the fried leeks to this bowl and whisk it well. Now, place the pie crust in a baking dish and add the egg-leek mixture to it. Spread it evenly.
  • Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake the Flamiche for 10 to 15 minutes. Reduce the temperature post that to 170 degrees Celsius and bake it until it is lightly brown in color on the top. Once bakes, take it out from the oven and place it on a plate. Slice and serve it warm to enjoy!
